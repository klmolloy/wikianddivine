Dionysus is the god of fertility and wine as well as a major/popular figure in Greco-Roman religion. The earliest reference to his worship appears in the 13th century BCE but his origins are unclear. There are mentions of him in Minoan Crete, Thrace and Greece and he is often portrayed as an Asiatic foreigner or a visitor from Ethopia. This forgieness is also shown in the myth of Dionysus' birth. His mother was Semele, a daughter of Cadmus (King of Thebes).
